Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv)(g), but excluding tax income and gain described in Treasury Reg. Section 1.704-1(b)(4)(i), and shall be decreased by (4) the amount of money distributed to such Member by the Company, (5) the fair market value of property distributed to such Member by the Company (net of liabilities secured by such distributed property that such Member is considered to assume or take subject to under Section 752 of the Code), (6) allocations to such Member of expenditures of the Company described in Section 705(a)(2)(B) of the Code, and (7) allocations of Company loss and deduction (or items thereof) including book loss and deduction described in Treasury Reg. Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv)(g), but excluding items described in (6) above and tax loss or deduction described in Treasury Reg. Section 1.704-1(b)(4)(i). Net profits and net losses of the Company from other than capital transactions, as of the end of any fiscal year or other period, shall be credited or charged to the capital accounts of the Members prior to any charge or credit to said capital accounts for net profits and net losses of the Company from capital transactions as of the end of such fiscal year or other period. 9. Books, Records and Tax Returns

11.1 Managers.

A. The Company shall conduct and manage its business and affairs in accordance with this Agreement and the laws of the State of Florida through a Manager or Managers (each a “Manager” and collectively, the “Managers”). The Company shall have at least one Manager at all times. Elections shall occur each year, on the anniversary of this Agreement, and shall be done by the Majority vote of the Members.

B. Except as otherwise set forth in this Agreement, the Managers shall vote on Company matters by Majority decision.

C. Any Manager may be removed at any time, with or without Cause, by the Majority vote of the Members excluding the Member or Manager in question, at a meeting called expressly for that purpose, or by the written Majority consent of the Members excluding the Member or Manager in question. Pursuant to 5.3 above, if a removal is with Cause, such removal shall affect the Manager's rights as a Member and constitute a withdrawal of a Member. For purposes of this section, “Cause” shall mean fraud, gross negligence, willful misconduct, breach of fiduciary duty not otherwise waived herein or elsewhere in writing, embezzlement, theft, material misrepresentation to other Managers, Members, or the Company, or a breach of the Manager's obligations under this Agreement which cannot be cured within 30 days from written notice of such breach. Notice of removal shall be served on the Manager and shall set forth the date on which the removal becomes effective. In the event that a Manager is also a member of any Member, such action referenced herein shall be considered as if it was undertaken by the Member. As such, should a Manager undertake any action which invokes removal for Cause, such action shall implicate the Member which the Manager represents as well.

D. The Members hereby designate {{Manager_1}} and {{Manager_2}} to serve as the Managers of the Company (each, a “Manager” and collectively, the “Managers”). The Company shall have at least one Manager at all times. Elections shall occur each year, on the anniversary of this Agreement, and shall be done by the Majority vote of the Members.

11.2 The Managers shall have responsibility for the day-to-day management of the business and affairs of the Company. Additionally, the Managers shall have the authority to sign and execute any and all documents related to the purchase or sale of real estate, including closing documents, closing statements, mortgage documents or otherwise.

11.3 Except as otherwise set forth in this Agreement, and in particular Paragraph 11.4 below, the Managers are hereby given power and authority to execute instruments on behalf of the Company and to otherwise bind the Company for obligations. No person, firm or corporation dealing with the Company shall be required to investigate the authority of the Managers or to secure the approval of or confirmation by the Members of any act of the Managers in connection with the business or affairs of the Company. Notwithstanding the foregoing, any Manager may execute a document or agreement on behalf of the Company, provided such document or agreement is in compliance with this Agreement and the voting requirements contained herein.

11.4 Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, the following restrictions shall apply to the Managers (Members shall also not be permitted to take any action without the necessary approval as set forth in this Agreement):

(i) The Majority Approval of the Members shall be required to (1) purchase, sell, exchange, lease, assign or otherwise transfer assets of the Company in an amount in greater than $5,000.00; (2) borrow money on behalf of the Company in an amount greater than $5,000.00; (3) establish any reserves for working capital repairs, replacements, improvements or any other purpose in an amount greater than $5,000.00; (4) confess a judgment against the Company; (5) settle, compromise or release, discharge or pay any claim, demand or debt; including claims for insurance in an amount greater than $5,000.00; (6) spend, execute a contract or enter into any transaction in an amount greater than or with a value over $5,000.00; (7) approve a merger or consolidation of the Company with or into any other limited liability company, corporation, partnership or other entity; and/or (8) change the nature or character of the business of the limited liability company.

11.5 The Managers shall be reimbursed by the Company for all direct out-of-pocket expenses that are incurred on behalf of the Company in the connection with the performance of their duties hereunder, without limitation amounts payable by the Managers for office, accounting, bookkeeping and other services, materials, facilities and professional and legal services rendered or furnished to the Company.

11.6 The Managers' duty of care in the discharge of the managing duties to the Company and the Members is limited to refraining from engaging in grossly negligent conduct, intentional misconduct, or a knowing violation of law. In discharging the duties of a Manager, the Managers shall be fully protected in relying in good faith upon the records of the Company and upon such information, opinions, reports or statements by other Managers, Members, agents or other persons as to matters the Manager reasonably believes are within such person’s professional or expert competence, including without limitation information, opinions, reports or statements as to the value or amount of the assets, liabilities, profits or losses of the Company or any other facts pertinent to the existence and amount of assets from which distributions to Members might properly be paid.

11.7 To the extent of the Company’s assets, and to the extent permitted by law, the Company shall indemnify and hold each Member and Manager harmless from and against all liability, claim, loss, damage or expense, including reasonable attorneys’ fees, incurred by the Member or Manager by reason of any act or omission of the Member or Manager made in good faith on behalf of the Company.

11.8 Except as expressly provided elsewhere in this Agreement, any decisions which are to be made by the Members, rather than the Manager, shall be made by the Majority vote or consent of the Members.

In the event that a Member is uncertain as to whether any information or material is Confidential Information such Member shall, prior to disseminating, disclosing or otherwise using in any way such information or material, ascertain from the Managers in writing whether such information or material is Confidential Information.

B. “Confidential Information” means all private (non-public) information and materials (the term materials is defined broadly and includes but is not limited to documents, video, audio and computer-stored data, imaging techniques, customer data and other intellectual property) concerning the Company, any Company subsidiaries, Customers and potential Customers, which the Company and/or any of such subsidiaries, Customers or potential Customers treat and/or designate as confidential, private, and/or proprietary, including, but not limited to, trade secrets, and other information and materials not generally known to the public. Confidential Information also includes, but is not limited to, all private (non-public) information and materials concerning or relating to the Company or any Company subsidiaries’ business and financial practices, marketing strategies, contracts, products, services, software, code, concepts, strategies, plans, Customer lists and records, contact lists and address books, finances, methods of servicing Customers, and proposals. Each Member acknowledges that information may be Confidential Information even though not expressly stamped or identified as such, and that the Member will treat all information in the general categories identified above as Confidential Information. Confidential Information shall not include information which is not private, not proprietary, or which is generally known in the respective industry.

C. Return of Confidential Information. Within two (2) business days of a Member's withdrawal from the Company or a Member's resignation or removal as a Manager or employee, or at any such other time upon the request of the Managers, such Member shall return all materials which contain Confidential Information to the Company, including all copies and summaries thereof. Each Member agrees that such Member's failure to comply with this paragraph shall cause damage to the Company the extent of which may be difficult or impossible to ascertain.

12. Assignment of Interests

12.1 Right of First Refusal.

A. Offer. Subject to Section 12, Section 13 and Section 14, any Member who wants to assign, transfer, sell, pledge or hypothecate its MPI (the “Selling Member”) shall first offer to sell his Shares to the other Members (the “Offerees”). The Offeror shall make the offer to sell by giving written notice to the Offerees (“Proposal”). The Proposal shall state the exact terms and price of the proposed sale.

B. Concurrence or Acceptance. The Offerees shall respond by giving notice within 30 calendar days of receiving the Proposal (the “Offer Period”). The Offeree’s response shall either accept the offer on the same terms and for the same price as in the Proposal or reject the offer. If more than one Offeree elects to purchase, the purchasers shall take the Offeror’s interest pro rata according to their respective Percentage Interests of Shares being sold by the Offeror. If the Offerees do not respond within the Offer Period or if the Offerees reject the offer, the Offeror may sell its interest to any other party approved by the Majority vote of the Members on the same terms and for the same price as in the Proposal for a period of 45 days after the end of the Offer Period. If the Offeror does not complete the sale of its interest within that 45-day period, the provisions of this Article 12 shall apply to any later sale or Proposal.

C. Rights of Buyer. A purchaser of the Selling Member's interest, if not already a Member, shall be an assignee but shall not become a Member unless it satisfies the requirements of Section 13.

12.2 Except as set forth within this Agreement, Members or other persons holding any interest in the Company may not assign, pledge, transfer or otherwise dispose of all or any part of his interest in the Company, including without limitation, the capital, profits or distributions of the Company to any person who is not a Member without the prior Majority consent of the {{Managed_type_plural}} in each instance.

12.3 No Member may voluntarily withdraw from the Company without the Majority vote or consent of the {{Managed_type_plural}} (other than the departing Member).

12.4 Notwithstanding anything contained herein, a Member may assign all or any part of such Member’s interest in the allocations and distributions of the Company to any of the following (collectively the “Permitted Assignees”): any other Member or any person, corporation, partnership or other entity as to which the Company has been notified of the assignment of such interest in the allocations and distributions of the Company. An assignment to a permitted assignee shall only entitle the permitted assignee to the allocations and distributions to which the assigned interest is entitled, unless such permitted assignee is already a Member or applies for admission to the Company and is admitted to the Company as a Member with the Majority vote of the {{Managed_type_plural}}.

12.5 An assignment, pledge, hypothecation, transfer or other disposition of all or any part of the interest of a Member in the Company or other person holding any interest in the Company in violation of the provisions hereof shall be null and void for all purposes.

12.6 No assignment, transfer or other disposition of all or any part of the interest of any Member permitted under this Agreement shall be binding upon the Company unless and until a duly executed and acknowledged counterpart of such assignment or instrument of transfer, in form and substance satisfactory to the {{Managed_type_plural}}, has been delivered to the Company.

12.7 No assignment or other disposition of any interest of any Member may be made if such assignment or disposition, alone or when combined with other transactions, would result in the termination of the Company within the meaning of Section 708 of the Internal Revenue Code or under any other relevant section of the Code or any successor statute. No assignment or other disposition of any interest of any Member may be made without an opinion of counsel satisfactory to the {{Managed_type_plural}} that such assignment or disposition is subject to an effective registration under, or exempt from the registration requirements of, the applicable State and Federal securities laws. No interest in the Company may be assigned or given to any person below the age of 21 years or to a person who has been adjudged to be insane or incompetent.

12.8 The Members and the Company shall be entitled to treat the record holder of the interest of a Member as the absolute owner thereof, and shall incur no liability by reason of distributions made in good faith to such record holder, unless and until there has been delivered to the {{Managed_type_plural}} assignment or other instrument of transfer and such other evidence as may be reasonably required by the Company to establish to the satisfaction of the {{Managed_type_plural}} that an interest has been assigned or transferred in accordance with this Agreement.

12.9 In the event that Members holding at least 50.1% of all the MPI of the Company desire to sell their entire MPI to a third party (the “Majority Selling Members”), and such sale has been approved by the Majority vote of the Members as required under this Agreement (an “Approved Sale”):

(i) Drag Along – the Managers shall have the right to require each other Member to participate in such Approved Sale, in which event the Managers shall notify each Member in writing of the terms of the Approved sale and each Member shall agree to transfer, and shall transfer, all of his, hers or its MPI to such third party purchaser on the same terms and conditions being offered to the Majority Selling Members in such Approved Sale, and each Member shall take all necessary or desirable actions in connection with the consummation of the Approved Sale as requested by the Managers (including, without limitation, executing and delivering any and all agreements, instruments and other documents executed by the Majority Selling Members, including any applicable purchase agreement, operating agreement and/or indemnification and/or contribution agreement); and

(ii) Tag Along – no such Approved Sale will be effective unless such third-party purchaser includes an offer to purchase and actually purchases (should such offer be accepted), on the same terms and conditions as those offered to the Majority Selling Members, all MPI tendered by the other Members (the “Tag Along Offer”). Such Tag Along Offer shall be accompanied by copies of any and all agreements, instruments and other documents which will be required to be executed by the other Members in connection with a sale of their MPI under the Tag Along Offer (and which shall be substantially identical to those agreements, instruments and other documents being executed by the Majority Selling Members), including any applicable purchase agreement, operating agreement and/or indemnification and/or contribution agreement. Such Tag Along Offer shall be held open for a period of thirty (30) days from the date it is delivered to the other Members. A Member may accept such Tag Along Offer by the delivery of written notice within the time period specified.

12.10 Deadlock. Any Member in the Company (the “Purchasing Member”) may, at any time after one year following the effective date of this Agreement, in the event of a deadlock or dispute that cannot be resolved as between the Members or via mediation as set forth in 19.6 below, purchase all (but not less than all) of the MPI in the Company of any other Member (“Selling Member”) under a Bona Fide Offer (as that term is defined below, which shall be no less than the value determined in 14.5 below), and under the specific terms and conditions provided for below. The Purchasing Member shall first give written notice to all other Members at least 30 days in advance of the proposed sale or transfer. The written notice must state the proposed purchase price and payment terms, if other than all cash. Provided that the payment terms are all cash, the Selling Member shall then have a 60 day period in which to decide whether to (1) accept the purchase price as offered and sell his MPI on the terms proposed (the “First Option”); or (2) purchase the MPI of the Purchasing Member on the terms proposed adjusted pro rata for any difference between the percentage interests of the Purchasing Member and Selling Member (the “Second Option”). The Selling Member shall give written notice of his decision to the Members within the 60-day notice period, and the purchase, if any, must be consummated within 60 days following delivery of the Selling Member's response. For purposes of this Paragraph, “Bona Fide Offer" means an offer in writing setting forth all relevant terms and conditions of the proposed purchase, but which must include provisions for the release of the Selling Member from all guarantees of debts of or personal liability for the debts of the Company, and which must be in compliance with Paragraph 14.5 below.

If a Bona Fide Offer (as defined immediately above) has been accepted by the Selling Member, the Purchasing Member must consummate the transaction within 60 calendar days following delivery of the Selling Member's acceptance. Should the Purchasing Member fail, for any reason, to consummate the transaction within such 60-calendar day period, then the Selling Member has the additional option of then purchasing the Membership Interest of the Purchasing Member on the terms proposed in the bona fide offer, less a 25% discount in the purchase price as a penalty imposed upon the Purchasing Member for failing to consummate the transaction. 14. Withdrawal Events Regarding Members

14.1 A Member who becomes incapacitated, as defined in this Section 14, has died, or has been removed due to any clause contained in this Agreement permitting such removal shall be referred to as the “Withdrawing Member”.

14.2 A Member’s death shall not cause the Company to dissolve. To be clear, the death of an owner or officer of a Member that is an entity shall not be considered the death of such Member.

14.3 A Member shall be deemed incapacitated as of the date it is judicially declared incompetent or insane or has appointed a legal guardian or conservator. The Successor’s interest shall be subject to the options described in Section 14.4 below. To be clear, the incapacity of an owner or officer of a Member that is an entity shall not be considered the death of such Member.

The Company’s decision to purchase a Successor's interest shall be within the discretion of a Majority of the remaining Members. This option shall be available for 60 calendar days after the date of death, departure, removal or incapacity and shall be exercised by giving notice to the Successor within that time period.

14.5 Valuation of a Member’s Interest. If (i) the Company decides to purchase a Successor’s interest in the Company as described in 14.4 above, (ii) the Company is purchasing a Member's interest in the event of a deadlock or dispute, or (iii) if a Member is removed from the Company as provided for in this Agreement (in all instances, a “Withdrawing Member”), the Company shall determine the value of the Withdrawing Member’s interest. The value shall be calculated as of the last day of the calendar month in which the Withdrawing Member dies or becomes incapacitated or the date the Company has determined to purchase the interest and remove the Member (the “Value Date”). The value shall be the amount that the Withdrawing Member would receive upon a sale of all of the Company’s assets as a going concern, taking into consideration the costs of the sale and assuming the assets would be sold at their fair market value. The Members shall work together in good faith to determine the fair market value. If the Members do not determine a fair market value within 45 days from the Value Date, two well-respected business valuation firms will be chosen to perform independent valuations of the Company using standard valuation methodologies (including but not limited to discounted cash flow, comparable transactions, publicly traded company comparable, etc.), with one business valuation firm chosen by the Company and the other chosen by the Successor or Withdrawing Member, as the case may be. To the extent that the two valuations are within 10% of each other, the fair market value will be the midpoint of the two valuations. To the extent that the two valuations vary by more than 10%, a third valuation firm (to be selected by the two valuation firms) shall conduct a valuation and the market value shall be the average of the two closest appraisals. Each party shall pay the cost of its appraiser valuation firm, and the parties shall equally divide the cost of the third valuation firm, should one be hired.

14.6 Removal for Cause. Except as otherwise provided for herein, any Member of the Company may be removed for cause, including but not limited to any criminal act, embezzlement, breach of fiduciary duty, or breach of duty of loyalty, or by the Majority vote or consent of all other Members of the Company excluding the Member that is the subject of removal upon (i) an abandonment of the Company (meaning that such Member has failed to attend meetings of the Members for five (5) consecutive unexcused absences or a lack of participation in the Company’s operations) or (ii) a failure to perform reasonable tasks befitting of a Member as agreed upon by the Members. Upon removal, the Company shall have the right to purchase the Withdrawing Member’s MPI, to be distributed pro-rata among the remaining Members. The valuation for the MPI in such instance shall be as set forth in Paragraph 14.5 above minus the cost incurred by the Company (including attorneys' fees) as a result of the act leading to removal of the Member.

15.1 The Company shall dissolve upon the occurrence of any of the following: the Majority election of the Members to dissolve the Company pursuant to a vote; the sale of all or substantially all of the assets of the Company as determined by a Majority of the Members; entry of a judicial decree of dissolution; or any other event which pursuant to this Agreement, as the same may hereafter be amended, shall cause a dissolution of the Company.

15.3 Promptly after the dissolution of the Company, the Liquidating Agent or Manager shall cause to be prepared and furnished to the Members a statement setting forth the assets and liabilities of the Company as of the date of dissolution. The Liquidating Agent or Manager, to the extent practicable, shall liquidate the assets of the Company as promptly as possible, but in an orderly and businesslike manner so as not to involve undue sacrifice and in accordance with the provisions of the Florida Limited Liability Company Act.

15.4 The proceeds of sale and all other assets of the Company shall be applied and distributed in the following order of priority: (a) to the payment of the expenses of liquidation and the debts and liabilities of the Company, other than debts and liabilities to Members; (b) to the payment of debts and liabilities to Members; (c) to the setting up of any reserves which the Liquidating Agent or Manager may deem necessary or desirable for any contingent or unforeseen liabilities or obligations of the Company, which reserves shall be paid over to an attorney-at-law admitted to practice in the State of Florida as escrowee, to be held for a period of two years for the purpose of payment of the aforesaid liabilities and obligations, at the expiration of which period the balance of such reserves shall be distributed as herein provided; and (d) to the Members in proportion to their respective capital accounts until each Member has received cash distributions equal to any positive balance in his capital account in accordance with the rules and requirements of Treasury Reg. Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(ii)(b).

15.5 The liquidation shall be complete within the period required by Treasury Reg. Section 1-704-1(b)(2)(ii)(b). If the Liquidating Agent or Manager shall determine that it is not practicable to liquidate all of the assets of the Company, the Liquidating Agent or Manager may retain assets having a fair market value equal to the amount by which the net proceeds of liquidated assets are insufficient to satisfy the debts and liabilities referred to above. 15.6 Upon compliance with the distribution plan, the Members shall cease to be such, and the Manager shall execute, acknowledge and cause to be filed such certificates and other instruments as may be necessary or appropriate to evidence the dissolution and termination of the Company.
