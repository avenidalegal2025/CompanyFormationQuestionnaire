This Shareholders’ Agreement (this “Agreement”) is dated effective as of {{effective_date}}th, 2026, by and among {{corp_name}}, a Florida corporation (the “Corporation”) and the Shareholder(s) set forth in Paragraph 4.2 and any addendum to this Agreement made a part hereof (the “Shareholders”).

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Shareholders are current owners beneficially and of record of all of the Corporation’s issued common stock, as set forth in Paragraph 4.2; and

WHEREAS, the Shareholders desire to promote their mutual interests with respect to the ownership of the Corporation and to provide for the harmonious and successful management and control of the Corporation, and recognize that to accomplish their objectives, they should restrict the transfer of their shares and impose certain other management limitations as set forth in this Agreement.

If the Offeror does not complete the sale of its interest within that 45-day period, the provisions of this Article 13 shall apply to any later sale or Proposal.

Purchase of Shareholder Interests upon Deadlock. In the event that a deadlock or dispute cannot be resolved as between the Shareholders, the Officers and/or the Board of Directors within a 15 day period, the Shareholders shall submit to a court certified mediation in {{county}} County, {{state}}. Following that, if a resolution cannot be reached, any Shareholder in the Corporation (the “Acquiring Shareholder”) may, within a sixty (60) day period following mediation, purchase all (but not less than all) of the Shares in the Corporation of any other Shareholder (“Acquired Shareholder”) under a Bona Fide Offer (as that term is defined below), and under the specific terms and conditions provided for below. The Acquiring Shareholder shall first give written notice to the Board and all other Shareholders at least 30 days in advance of the proposed sale or transfer. The written notice must state the proposed purchase price and payment terms, if other than all cash. Provided that the payment terms are all cash, the Acquired Shareholder shall then have the notice period in which to decide whether to (1) accept the purchase price as offered and sell his Shares on the terms proposed (the “First Option”); or (2) purchase the Shares of the Acquiring Shareholder on the terms proposed adjusted pro rata for any difference between the percentage interests of the Acquiring Shareholder and Acquired Shareholder (the “Second Option”). The Acquired Shareholder shall give written notice of his decision to the Board of Directors and all other Shareholders in the Corporation within the 30 day notice period, and the purchase, if any, must be consummated within 60 days following delivery of the Acquired Shareholder’s response. For purposes of this Agreement, “Bona Fide Offer" means an offer in writing setting forth all relevant terms and conditions of the proposed purchase, but which must include provisions for the release of the Acquired Shareholder from all guarantees of debts or personal liability for the debts of the Corporation.

ARTICLE XIV: DEATH, INCAPACITY OR DIVORCE OF SHAREHOLDER

Withdrawing Shareholder’s Status. A Shareholder who becomes incapacitated, as defined in Section 14.1(b) or has died, shall be referred to as a “Withdrawing Shareholder.”

Death of a Shareholder. The estate of a deceased Shareholder and the person entitled to succeed to the Corporation interest of a deceased Shareholder under the decedent’s will or the laws of intestate succession shall be referred to as a “Successor.” On the death of a Shareholder, the Successor shall become a Shareholder, with all the rights and obligations of the deceased Shareholder except that its interest shall be subject to the options described in Section 14.1(d) below.

Incapacity of a Shareholder. A Shareholder shall be deemed incapacitated as of the date it undergoes bankruptcy, is judicially declared incompetent or insane, or has appointed a legal guardian or conservator. The trustee in bankruptcy, legal guardian, or conservator shall be referred to as the Successor. On the incapacity of a Shareholder, its Successor shall become a Shareholder except that its interest shall be subject to the options described in Section 14.1(d) below.

Divorce. Except as otherwise set forth in this Agreement, upon the filing of a petition for dissolution of marriage or legal separation of a Shareholder, the divorcing Shareholder shall give notice to the Corporation immediately in order to start the option period as described in 14.1 (d). Any such Involuntary Assignee shall be bound by 14.1(d) below.

Successor’s Interest.

The Successor may retain the interest or sell the interest. The Corporation shall have the first option to purchase the Successor or Involuntary Assignee’s interest within 60 days from the death, incapacity or divorce of the Withdrawing Shareholder. The Corporation’s decision to exercise its option to purchase shall be within the sole discretion of a Majority vote of the Board of Directors and the Shareholders. The Corporation must notify the Successor of the decision to acquire such Shares in writing within 60 days.

The Corporation shall further have the option to purchase the Shares from a divorcing Shareholder's spouse or any Involuntary Assignee; such divorced spouse or Involuntary Assignee shall be required to sell upon the Corporation's exercise of its option. The Corporation’s decision to exercise its option to purchase shall be within the sole discretion of a Majority vote of the Board of Directors. This option shall be available for 60 calendar days after the divorce or Involuntary Transfer and shall be exercised by giving notice to the Corporation and Involuntary Assignee or spouse within that time period.

Valuation of Successor’s Interest. If the Corporation decides to purchase an Involuntary Assignee, Divorcing Shareholder’s or Successor’s interest in the Corporation as described in Section 14.1(d) above, the value of such Interest shall be calculated as of the last day of the calendar month in which the Withdrawing Shareholder or Involuntary Assignee dies, becomes incapacitated, divorces or the Shares are Involuntarily Transferred (the “Value Date”). The value shall be the pro-rata amount that the Withdrawing Shareholder or Involuntary Assignee would receive upon a sale of the Corporation as a going concern at fair market value. If the determination of value is not made within 60 calendar days of the Value Date or if the Successor, Assignee or Divorcing Shareholder reasonably disagree with the valuation, then the Corporation and the Involuntary Assignee or Successor shall appoint an independent third party valuation expert. The cost of the appraisal shall be divided equally amongst the Successor and the Corporation. If the parties are unable to agree on an appraiser, each shall choose its own appraiser, and the two appraisers shall agree to select a third. The valuation shall be an average of the third appraiser's valuation and the appraisal that came closest to the third appraiser. Each party shall pay the costs for its own appraiser, and the parties shall equally divide the cost of the third appraiser.

Payment Upon Withdrawal. If the Corporation purchases the Involuntary Assignee or Successor's interest, the Corporation shall pay the Involuntary Assignee or Successor the stated amount under such terms as may be determined by the Board of Directors in their reasonable discretion, provided that such payment terms do not exceed three (3) years in length.

If the Corporation fails to notify the Divorcing Shareholder, Successor or Involuntary Assignee of its decision within the 60-day Period, or if the Corporation notifies such parties that it will not purchase the Shares, the Divorcing Shareholder, Successor, or Involuntary Assignee shall become a shareholder, provided that such party becomes a party to this Agreement. Once such Successor or Involuntary Assignee becomes a Shareholder, they shall have the rights and limitations set for in this Agreement for such Class of Shares. Alternatively, such Divorcing Shareholder, Successor or Involuntary Assignee may at this time elect to sell the Shares to a third party, at the fair market value (but no less than any offer received from or made to the Corporation, if applicable) and pursuant to Section 13 above, provided the Shareholders by Majority approve such new shareholder. All future attempted transfers will be subject to this Shareholders' Agreement and require the Majority approval of the Shareholders, except that the Personal Representative may transfer the Shares to the deceased Shareholder's beneficiaries, provided the beneficiaries become parties to this Shareholders' Agreement and become Shareholders for purposes of this Shareholders' Agreement.
